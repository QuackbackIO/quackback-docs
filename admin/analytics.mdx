# Measure feedback with analytics

Stop guessing what's working. The analytics dashboard shows you how feedback flows through your workspace, where users engage most, and how quickly your team responds.

## Overview

Access analytics from **Admin → Analytics** in the sidebar. The dashboard is organized into four sections:

| Section | What it answers |
|---------|-----------------|
| Volume | How much feedback are you receiving? |
| Status transitions | How does feedback move through your workflow? |
| Engagement | Who's participating and how? |
| Response time | How fast is your team responding? |

## Feedback volume

The volume chart shows how many posts your workspace receives over time. Toggle between daily, weekly, and monthly views to spot trends.

### Volume by board

Break down volume by [board](/admin/boards) to see which areas of your product generate the most feedback. A stacked bar chart shows each board's contribution to total volume.

### Volume by status

See how posts are distributed across [statuses](/admin/statuses). The status breakdown reveals bottlenecks — if most posts sit in "Open", your team may need to triage more frequently.

<Tip>
A growing backlog of "Open" posts is normal as your user base grows. Focus on the ratio of new posts to triaged posts rather than the absolute count.
</Tip>

## Status transitions

Track how feedback moves through your workflow. The transition view shows:

- **Flow diagram** — a visual path from initial status to resolution
- **Average time in status** — how long posts spend at each stage
- **Transition counts** — how many posts moved between specific statuses in the selected period

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|-------------------|
| Avg. time in Open | How quickly your team starts triaging |
| Avg. time in Under Review | How long evaluation takes |
| Avg. time in Planned | How long items wait before development starts |
| Open → Closed rate | Percentage of feedback declined without implementation |

<Note>
Status transition data starts tracking from the moment you create your workspace. Historical data before analytics was enabled is not included.
</Note>

## User engagement

Understand who's participating and how actively.

### Active voters

Track the number of unique users who voted in the selected period. A rising voter count indicates growing community involvement.

### Top contributors

A ranked list of users by total activity — posts submitted, votes cast, and comments left. Use this to identify your most engaged users.

### Comment activity

See comment volume over time, split by:

- **User comments** — feedback from portal users
- **Team replies** — responses from your team members
- **Internal notes** — private comments between team members

<Tip>
The ratio of team replies to user comments is a good proxy for responsiveness. Aim for at least one team reply per post with comments.
</Tip>

## Response time

Measure how quickly your team engages with feedback.

| Metric | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| Time to first response | Duration from post creation to the first team comment |
| Time to resolution | Duration from post creation to "Complete" or "Closed" status |
| Median response time | The midpoint response time (less skewed by outliers than average) |

Response times are displayed as median values by default. You can toggle to show averages.

<Warning>
Posts without a team response or resolution are excluded from these calculations. Filter by board or segment to compare response times across different feedback categories.
</Warning>

## Filter analytics

Narrow your analytics view using the filter bar at the top of the dashboard.

### Date range

Select a preset range or define a custom one:

| Preset | Period |
|--------|--------|
| Last 7 days | Previous week |
| Last 30 days | Previous month |
| Last 90 days | Previous quarter |
| Last 12 months | Previous year |
| Custom | Pick start and end dates |

### Board filter

Select one or more [boards](/admin/boards) to scope all metrics to feedback in those categories.

### Segment filter

Choose a [user segment](/admin/segments) to see analytics for a specific customer group. Compare how enterprise customers engage versus free-tier users, or see response times for your highest-value accounts.

<Tip>
Combine board and segment filters for granular insights. For example, filter to "Feature Requests" board + "Enterprise" segment to see what your largest customers ask for most.
</Tip>

## Export reports

Download analytics data for use in spreadsheets, presentations, or external BI tools.

1. Apply the filters you want (date range, board, segment)
2. Click **Export** in the top-right corner
3. Choose a format:

| Format | Best for |
|--------|----------|
| CSV | Spreadsheets and data analysis |
| PDF | Presentations and stakeholder reports |

Exports include all metrics visible on the dashboard for the selected filters and date range.

<Note>
Exports reflect the currently applied filters. Double-check your filter bar before exporting to make sure you're capturing the right data.
</Note>
