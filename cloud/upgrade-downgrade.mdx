title: Upgrades and downgrades
description: How tier changes work, what happens when you hit a limit, and what survives a downgrade.
---

## Hitting a limit

When you try to do something that exceeds your tier's limit (e.g. create
the 101st post on Starter), Quackback returns a clear error message with
the limit, your current usage, and a CTA to upgrade. Existing posts are
unaffected — you can keep editing, voting on, and commenting on what
you have.

### Posts: archive is the relief valve

The Starter post cap counts non-archived posts. If you hit 100 posts and
want headroom, archive older items — they remain readable in the archive
view but no longer count toward the cap. This makes Starter genuinely
usable for many teams indefinitely.

## Upgrading

Upgrades take effect immediately. New limits are pushed to your
workspace within a few seconds. We prorate the upgrade against your
current billing cycle.

## Downgrading

When you downgrade (e.g. Pro → Starter), **existing data stays
accessible**. Boards, posts, seats, integrations all keep working. You
just won't be able to create new ones above the new tier's limits until
you upgrade again.

We never delete your data on downgrade. The over-limit state itself
is the upgrade pressure if your team wants to add more.

### Examples

- **You have 8 boards on Pro and downgrade to Starter (3-board limit).**
  All 8 boards stay live. You can edit and use them. You cannot create
  new boards until you upgrade or delete enough boards to be under the
  cap.
- **You have 6 team seats on Pro and downgrade to Starter (2-seat limit).**
  All 6 admins/members keep access. You cannot invite new admins/members
  until you free up seats.
- **You're using AI features regularly on Pro and downgrade to Starter
  (no AI).** AI features stop working immediately on downgrade. Existing
  AI-generated summaries on posts remain visible.

## Trials

Pro and Scale both include a 14-day free trial. No credit card required
to start. At the end of the trial, your workspace downgrades to Starter
unless you've added a payment method.
