---
title: AI token budget
description: Understanding the aiTokensPerMonth quota on Pro and Scale.
---

Pro and Scale include a monthly **token budget** that powers every AI
feature: post summaries, duplicate / merge suggestions, sentiment, and
feedback extraction from connected sources.

We bill by tokens because that's the actual cost driver — a 100-token
summary is ~10× cheaper than a 1k-token batch assessment. Token-based
pricing means you get more value out of small calls and the gauge maps
directly to our underlying LLM spend.

## Quotas by tier

| Tier | Tokens / month | Approx. equivalent* |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | — | AI not included |
| Pro | 5,000,000 | ~5,000 summaries |
| Scale | 50,000,000 | ~50,000 summaries |

\* Rough estimate based on a typical post summary using around 1k tokens
(input + output combined). Merge assessments and sentiment use less;
long-form extraction calls use more.

## What does and doesn't count

**Counts toward your budget**:
- Every successful LLM (chat-completion) call: summaries, merge assessments,
  sentiment classification, feedback extraction
- Both input and output tokens combined (`total_tokens` from the LLM provider)

**Does not count**:
- Embeddings (semantic search, merge prefilter, Help Center search)
- Vector similarity search and full-text search
- Failed/retried calls — only the final successful attempt is billed
- Pure SQL operations (analytics aggregations, etc.)

## What happens when you hit the cap

AI features pause until the start of the next billing month, or you can
upgrade your plan for a larger budget. Existing AI-generated artifacts
(summaries, sentiment scores) remain visible — only new AI work is
blocked.
